French Onion Meatloaf with Creamed Peas and Potatoes

Cuisine: American

Meal Type: Dinner

Prep Time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 yellow onion, halved and thinly sliced
  • 2 packets beef broth concentrate
  • 2 lbs ground beef
  • 2 oz panko breadcrumbs (about ½ cup)
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 tsp steak seasoning
  • 4 oz shredded fontina cheese
  • 4 tbsp butter, divided
  • 2 large eggs
  • 4 tsp all-purpose flour, divided
  • 1½ cups water (for onion gravy)
  • 1 lb baby red potatoes, quartered
  • 1 cup shelled English peas
  • 1 cup milk
  • Kosher salt and ground pepper
  • Neutral oil
  • 2 tsp garlic, finely chopped

Instructions

  1. Brown Onions: Preheat the oven to 450°F with a rack in the upper third. Melt 2 tbsp butter in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Add the sliced onions and season with salt and pepper. Reduce heat to medium and cook, stirring occasionally, until golden brown (about 12 minutes). Add a drizzle of oil if the saucepan becomes dry.
  2. Make Onion Gravy: Stir 4 tsp flour into the onions and cook for 1 minute. Add the beef broth concentrate and 1½ cups water. Bring to a boil and cook until the liquid thickens to a thin gravy consistency (5–7 minutes). Season with salt and pepper. Transfer gravy to a bowl and set aside. Wipe out the saucepan for later use.
  3. Make Meatloaf Mixture: In a large bowl, combine ground beef, garlic, panko breadcrumbs, Dijon mustard, steak seasoning, 2 tsp salt, and eggs. Mix gently until just combined.
  4. Bake Meatloaves: Form the meat mixture into 4 oval-shaped patties (about 5 inches long) and place them in a large ovenproof skillet. Bake on the upper oven rack until firm to the touch and the internal temperature reaches 160°F, about 20 minutes. Carefully spoon off any accumulated fat.
  5. Make Creamed Peas and Potatoes: Place the potatoes in a large pot and cover with salted water. Bring to a boil, reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer until tender (about 15–20 minutes). Drain. Meanwhile, bring a medium saucepan of water to a boil. Add the peas and simmer until just tender (6–7 minutes). Drain and set aside. In the same saucepan, melt 1 tbsp butter over medium heat. Stir in 1 tbsp flour to form a thick paste. Gradually whisk in the milk, stirring constantly until slightly thickened. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Add the potatoes and peas and simmer, stirring often, until heated through (about 5 minutes).
  6. Finish & Serve: Switch the oven to broil. Spoon onion gravy over the meatloaves, then top with shredded fontina. Broil on the upper oven rack until the gravy is bubbling and the cheese is melted (2–3 minutes). Serve the meatloaves with creamed peas and potatoes on the side. Enjoy!

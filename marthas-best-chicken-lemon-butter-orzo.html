Martha's Best Chicken & Lemon Butter Orzo

Cuisine: American

Meal Type: Dinner

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 chicken broth concentrate packet
  • 16 oz boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ¼ tsp onion powder
  • ¼ tsp dried thyme
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • 3 oz orzo
  • ¾ oz Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 1 lemon (zest and juice)
  • 5 oz baby spinach
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • Kosher salt and ground pepper

Instructions

  1. Prep Ingredients: Preheat the oven to 400ºF with a rack in the center. Finely chop the onion. Finely chop 1 tsp garlic. In a bowl, stir together the chicken broth concentrate and 1¼ cups warm water.
  2. Season & Sear Chicken: Pat the chicken dry and season all over with paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, dried thyme, black pepper, and a pinch of salt. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a medium ovenproof skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and sear until well browned on both sides, 2–3 minutes per side. Transfer the chicken to a plate (it will not be cooked through). Return the skillet to the stove.
  3. Start Orzo: Heat 2 tbsp butter in the same skillet over medium heat. Add the onions and a pinch of salt; cook, stirring, until softened (3–4 minutes). Add the chopped garlic and cook until fragrant (1 minute). Add the orzo and cook, stirring, until lightly toasted (1–2 minutes).
  4. Bake Orzo & Chicken: To the skillet with orzo, add the chicken broth mixture and ½ tsp salt. Bring to a simmer over medium-high heat. Off the heat, place the chicken on top of the orzo. Cover the skillet with foil and bake on the center oven rack until the broth is mostly absorbed, the orzo is al dente, and the chicken is cooked through (165ºF internally), about 15 minutes.
  5. Add Spinach & Parmesan: Meanwhile, finely grate Parmesan if needed. Into a small bowl, finely grate 1 tsp lemon zest and squeeze 1 tbsp lemon juice. When the chicken and orzo are done, transfer the chicken to a cutting board. Stir the spinach into the orzo in batches until wilted. Stir in the lemon zest, lemon juice, and half of the Parmesan.
  6. Finish & Serve: Season the orzo to taste with salt and pepper. Serve the chicken with the orzo and sprinkle the remaining Parmesan over the top. Garnish with any remaining lemon wedges for squeezing. Enjoy!

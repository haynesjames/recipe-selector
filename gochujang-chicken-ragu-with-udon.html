Gochujang Chicken Ragu with Udon

Cuisine: Asian

Meal Type: Dinner

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 scallions, thinly sliced
  • 1 tbsp garlic, finely chopped
  • 4.5 oz curly kale (about half a bunch), coarsely chopped
  • 16 oz ground chicken
  • 2 tbsp neutral oil
  • 1 oz gochujang (about 2 tbsp)
  • ¼ cup tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp tamari soy sauce
  • 1½ cups water
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 7 oz udon noodles
  • 2 tsp sesame seeds
  • 2 tsp fresh cilantro leaves, torn
  • Kosher salt and ground pepper

Instructions

  1. Bring a medium saucepan of salted water to a boil. Trim scallions, then thinly slice. Finely chop 1 tbsp garlic. Remove half of the kale leaves from stems, discard stems, and coarsely chop leaves.
  2. Heat 2 tbsp neutral oil in a medium skillet over medium-high heat. Add the ground chicken and a pinch of salt and pepper. Use a spoon to break the meat into large pieces. Cook without stirring until the chicken is browned on one side, 3–5 minutes.
  3. To the skillet with browned chicken, add the scallions, chopped garlic, gochujang, and tomato paste. Cook, stirring, until the scallions are softened and the garlic is fragrant, about 2 minutes.
  4. Stir in tamari soy sauce, 1½ cups water, and 1 tbsp sugar. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce heat to medium-low. Add the chopped kale and cook, stirring often, until the sauce is thickened, the kale is tender, and the chicken is cooked through, 5–7 minutes. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  5. Add udon noodles to the boiling water and cook, stirring occasionally, until just tender, about 5 minutes. Drain the noodles well, rinse under cold water, and drain again.
  6. Add the noodles to the skillet with the ragu and toss to coat. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. Serve topped with torn cilantro leaves and sesame seeds. Enjoy!

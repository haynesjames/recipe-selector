Korean Beef Quesadillas with Cucumber Salad

Cuisine: Korean-Mexican Fusion

Meal Type: Dinner

Prep Time: 35 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 cucumber, peeled and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 tbsp garlic, finely chopped
  • 2 tsp white wine vinegar (or apple cider vinegar)
  • 2 tsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 1 tsp tamari soy sauce
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ½ tsp kosher salt
  • 2 oz mayonnaise
  • 2 oz gochujang (Korean chili paste), divided
  • 16 oz ground beef
  • 4 oz shredded cheddar-jack cheese
  • 6 (6-inch) flour tortillas
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil (for browning beef)
  • 3 tbsp water (for glaze)
  • 1 tbsp sugar (for glaze)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil (for glaze)
  • 1 tsp tamari soy sauce (for glaze)
  • Kosher salt and ground pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Make Cucumber Salad: In a medium bowl, toss cucumber pieces with 2 tsp garlic, 2 tsp vinegar, 2 tsp sesame seeds, 1 tsp tamari, 1 tsp sesame oil, 1 tsp sugar, and ½ tsp salt. Set aside until ready to serve.
  2. Make Sauce & Glaze: In a small bowl, combine mayonnaise, half of the gochujang, 1 tbsp water, 1 tsp sugar, and ½ tsp sesame oil. Set aside for serving. In another small bowl, combine the remaining gochujang, tamari, 3 tbsp water, 1 tbsp sugar, and 1 tsp sesame oil. Set aside as the glaze for the beef.
  3. Brown Beef: Heat 1 tbsp neutral oil in a medium skillet over high heat. Add the ground beef and cook, breaking it up into smaller pieces, until browned and cooked through, 4–5 minutes. Stir in the remaining garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add the glaze and cook, scraping up browned bits from the skillet, until the beef is well coated and the skillet is mostly dry, about 1–2 minutes. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  4. Assemble Quesadillas: Preheat the broiler with a rack in the top position. Brush one side of each tortilla with neutral oil and place oiled side down on a rimmed baking sheet. Divide the beef mixture evenly among the tortillas, spooning it onto one half of each. Top with shredded cheddar-jack cheese and fold the tortillas in half.
  5. Broil Quesadillas: Broil on the top oven rack until the cheese is melted and the tortillas are golden brown, rotating the baking sheet and flipping the quesadillas halfway through, about 2–4 minutes. Watch closely to avoid burning. Let the quesadillas cool for 5 minutes before cutting into wedges, if desired.
  6. Serve: Serve the quesadillas with cucumber salad and the prepared gochujang sauce. Garnish with the remaining sesame seeds. Enjoy!

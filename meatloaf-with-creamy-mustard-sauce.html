Meatloaf with Creamy Mustard Sauce

Cuisine: American

Meal Type: Dinner

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tsp garlic, finely chopped
  • ½ lb carrots, thinly sliced on an angle
  • 4 oz snap peas, trimmed
  • 2 tsp fresh tarragon, coarsely chopped
  • 16 oz grass-fed ground beef
  • 3 tbsp panko breadcrumbs
  • 2½ tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1½ tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 oz sour cream (about 2 tbsp)
  • 1 large egg
  • Olive oil
  • Butter
  • Red wine vinegar (or apple cider vinegar)
  • Kosher salt and ground pepper

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 450°F with a rack in the center. Finely chop the yellow onion and heat 2 tsp olive oil in a medium skillet over medium-high heat. Add the onion and 1 tsp chopped garlic, stirring occasionally, until softened (4–5 minutes). Transfer to a bowl to cool. Reserve the skillet for Step 4.
  2. Scrub the carrots and slice them thinly on an angle. Trim the snap peas as needed. Pick and coarsely chop 2 tsp tarragon leaves (discard the stems).
  3. To the bowl with the onions and garlic, add the ground beef, panko breadcrumbs, 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, 1 large egg, 1¼ tsp salt, and a few grinds of pepper. Knead to combine. Shape into a 6-inch long meatloaf and place on a rimmed baking sheet. Bake on the center rack until browned and firm, about 20 minutes or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  4. To the reserved skillet, add the carrots and 2 tbsp water. Bring to a boil, cover, and cook until softened (3–4 minutes). Add the snap peas and ½ tbsp olive oil. Cook, stirring, until water evaporates and vegetables are tender and browned (2–3 minutes). Stir in tarragon, 1 tbsp butter, and ½ tsp vinegar. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Transfer veggies to a platter. Heat 1 tsp olive oil in the same skillet over medium heat. Add the remaining 1 tsp garlic, stirring until fragrant (about 30 seconds). Add 1½ tsp Dijon mustard, 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, and ¼ cup water. Whisk and cook until slightly thickened (2–3 minutes). Stir in sour cream and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Slice the meatloaf and serve alongside the veggies with mustard cream sauce spooned over top. Enjoy!
